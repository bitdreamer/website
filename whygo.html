Why on Earth would you go to an all women's college for Computer Science?

by Barrett Koster, professor of Computer Science and stuff

Will it help you get a job?  Yes.  Will you learn better among just women?  Yes.  Is this a convent?  Oh please -- the boys are just down the street.   Do you want to become somebody?  Do you?

Did the tech bubble burst your job?  

Heck no.  The financial risk takers took a hit when they got caught over-speculating, but the technology has never gone away.   Your phone, your MP3 player, your computer .... somebody has to make all this junk work, lots of somebodies.   There are more gadgets and technology every day, and companies wonder how they are going to get enough people to handle it all.

CareerBuilder.com reports that "College students graduating in 2004-05 with a math- or science-related degree are likely to earn significantly higher starting pay than their peers in liberal arts disciplines, according to a spring salary survey by the National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE)."  (See article.)  If your main concern is about getting a job, then Computer Science or Computer Information Systems is still ahead of the English majors.

But it's more than a job -- get the fun back in your computer.

Do you remember when you thought the computer was kinda cool, before your brother hogged it, before the guys at your highschool took over and turned everything about them into either sex or explosions?  Did you know the first programmer was a woman?  And there have been lots since.  Computers are just as much for women as men.   But studies have shown that women demand more meaning from their programming; women want to have a purpose where guys are content with doing things "for the hell of it".  Is a mixed gender school going to change their assignments to suit you?  Some have, but not many.  We at Meredith obviously strive to make the work relevant to women. 

And we usually have a good time in class.   Computer are such odd things, it's easy to find humor in just about everything.  The history is so recent that the heros and the villians are still in the news.  And the programs themselves -- failure is frequently spectacular or at least really comical, and success is as sweet as it gets, either doing it yourself or sharing with a team.

Are you thinking of majoring in Art or French or Biology or Math?  Have you tried computers?  Some of my best students are into Art or French, but many discover  they love computers too late to major.  Maybe computers won't work for you, but you really should at least try it.  And downloading music is a good skill, but if that's all the fartther you've gotten, there's a new world waiting for you.

Are you a 2nd class citizen?

Do you want to be able to build or upgrade your computer without some guy elbowing you out of the way?  Do you want to be able to ask questions in class without the guys acting like they already know it (and trust me, it's acting)?  If you are a woman and have never been in a place that is for women, you may never have known what it is like to be a first class citizen.  As a man, I have never known a day without it, and I'll tell you it makes a difference. 

When I interviewed at Meredith, I could tell the women thought of themselves differently before I even got out of my car.   The way the students walked across the street said that they belonged in a way that I'd never seen women do before.  Graduates of women's colleges occupy a disproportionate number of the postions held by women in government and as exectutives in industry.   The irony of some parents sending their daughters here to be sheltered is that the daughters become less likely to buy the traditional role as asisstant people.  At mixed gender schools, how many women become student government president, honor council chair or even tech assistant?  But at Meredith,  they all are.  You get used to it, the feeling of being top dog, and it's not something you give up when you leave. 

In the end .....

Choosing a college is hard.  Since you've read this far, you're at least considering Meredith.   Why don't you come for a visit -- party in the Bit Lab!
