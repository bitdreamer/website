Student Opportunities (research grants, internships, mentoring ....)

CRA is the Computer Research Association.  The sub-group on women, CRA-W, has several programs ... here are just a few of pointers:

  • CREU:  grants of $1000-$2000 to groups of undergraduate women students for research projects in computer science.  Barrett Koster knows the head of this project and has helped her review proposals, so he can help you make a GOOD project proposal.  Proposals are due some time in April.   The funding rate is better than most, so your odds are good.  Apply!  See the site for details, and then talk with Dr. Koster.
  • DMP (Distributed Mentor Project).  Students are given a full ride 10-week summer research assistantship at a major research institution.   All mentors are female, and students get to try out working in a real environment on real problems.  Three of Dr. Koster's students have been on one of these, and they each had a blast.
  • There are a number of other links on the CRA-W site, like Systers (nominally, women system programmers).  Have a look.
